GRE

10,000

Gradutae Reward Exam(GRE), is a test written by Prospective Graduate students, applying for Master's courses in multiple Universities across USA, Canada, and other European countries.

If you're looking to study Graduate courses (Masters or MBA) in top Universities across the world, submitting GRE scores is a mandatory requirement. Universities take GRE Scores to be a common indicator of a student's Analytical, Logical, and Verbal Reasoning Skills. This test, conducted by a non-profit educational assessment and research organization called ETS, was designed to reflect a student's critcal thinking abilities.

5,000

Test of English as a Foreign Langugae(TOEFL), is a computer based english language test which tests a student's English Reading, Writing, Listening, and Speaking skills.

For almost every foreign University where courses are taught in Engllish, it's mandatory for a student whose native language is not English, to submit minimum scores in a certified English language test. TOEFL is one of the three popular tests, the other two being IELTS and PTE.

IELTS

3,000

The International English Language Testing System(IELTS), is a paper based test designed to assess Test Taker's English communnication skills in Reading, Writing, Listening and Speaking tasks. The Exam is scored out of 9 Bands, to measure the level of proficiency in English. Band score 1 is attributed as Non-Speaker and 9 as Expert.

There are two types in the IELTS- General and Academic. Test takers who are applying to Universities are required to write Academic version, where as those who are migrating for work need to write the General Version.

PTE

4,000

The Pearson Test for English, is generally take by Non-native English speakers to prove their Reading, Writing, Listening and Speaking skills, to the Universities where courses are taught in the English Language. PTE is an Online based Test

To complete a PTE Academic test, you will need to attend a secure Pearson test center. You will use a computer and headset to listen to, read, and respond to questions. During the three-hour test session there will be three main parts to the test: speaking and writing (together), listening and reading.
